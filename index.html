Research

Spatial omics of brain disorders

We develop and apply spatial omics approaches to map molecular and cellular changes in healthy and diseased brain states.

Neural circuits of memory and decision-making

We investigate how neural circuits encode experience, guide behavior, and become maladaptive in neurological and psychiatric disorders.

Multimodal brain mapping

We combine spatial transcriptomics, circuit neuroscience, imaging, and computational analysis to understand brain function across scales.

People

Yiming Zhou, Ph.D.

Principal Investigator

Yiming is currently a faculty member with the Interdisciplinary Research Center on Biology and Chemistry at Chinese Academy of Sciences, Shanghai. ​Yiming completed his Ph.D. in System Neuroscience at Fudan University, where he studied the neuronal circuitry mechanisms of learning and memory under the guidance of Dr. Lan Ma. After his PhD, Yiming joined Broad Institute as the postdoc under the mentorship of Dr. Xiao Wang, where he developed technologies in spatial circuits mapping. The Zhou Lab develops new spatial technologies to study learning and memory and decision-making under healthy and disease state at molecular, synaptic, cellular and circuit levels.
